RISE FOR JUSTICE

Donghak Peasant Rebellion | Korea | 1894

END CORRUPTION • PROTECT THE PEOPLE
Message to the People: We are farmers, workers, and families pushed to the edge. Officials take our rice, our wages, and our dignity. We rise for fairness and for the right to live with respect.

Why We Rebel

  • Corrupt local officials abuse power and steal from villages.
  • Unfair taxes keep peasants trapped in poverty.
  • Tonghak believers face persecution and discrimination.
  • A system built for elites ignores ordinary people.

Our Vision

  • Equal treatment and dignity for every person.
  • An honest government that serves the people.
  • Protection of our communities and beliefs.
  • Justice that reaches the countryside and not only the capital.

How We Fought

  • Local militias organized village by village.
  • Key towns were taken to force the government to listen.
  • Major battles were fought at Hwangtojae and Jeonju.
  • Foreign military intervention overwhelmed the movement.

Call to Action

Stand with the people. Share the truth. Reject corruption. Support fairness and the right to live with dignity.

Choose the people, not the thieves in power.

Sources Used

This project uses Korea, Old and New by Eckert et al., an academic database entry on the 1894 Tonghak rebellion, and background material on Ch’ŏndogyo beliefs.

Face of the Movement

Jeon Bong-jun, leader of the 1894 Donghak peasant uprising.

Quick Timeline

  • Early 1894: Uprising begins in Gobu.
  • April 1894: Battle of Hwangtojae.
  • May 1894: Rebels capture Jeonju.
  • November 1894: Final defeat at Ugeumchi.

Enemy

  • Corrupt Joseon officials and the Yi dynasty system.
  • Later suppression by Japanese military forces.
